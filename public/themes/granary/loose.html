How loose works.

It's three steps and a scale. The point is you only buy what you'll eat — and you leave the packaging behind.

1

Bring jars (or borrow ours)

Mason jars, takeout containers, the bag the previous bag came in — all welcome. We weigh and label your container at the front so its weight is subtracted automatically. Or borrow one of our deposit-return jars for $1.

2

Fill what you actually need

Use the scoops and dispensers at each bin. There's no minimum — fill a teaspoon of nutmeg, a cup of flour, half a kilo of oats. Whatever this week's recipe needs.

3

Weigh, label, pay

Our scales do the math at the front counter. Each bin has a 4-digit code; punch it in, weigh, print the sticker, pay. We'll wash and refill our jars after; yours go straight home.

The math.

Every bin price is per 100 grams of food, with no packaging cost built in. So 250g of basmati rice is exactly 2.5x what 100g costs — no rounding, no surprises.

For most pantry staples, our prices end up being 20-40% cheaper than packaged equivalents at the supermarket. The savings get bigger the more you cook from scratch.

About the food.

We list the source farm or co-op next to every bin. Most of what's on the floor was grown within 250km of Your Store Name; the rest is single-origin and traceable to the village.

Bins get refilled small-and-often, which means nothing sits long enough to go stale. Spices get refilled monthly, flours weekly, oats and grains in larger but more frequent batches.

Common questions.

What if I forget to bring a jar?

No problem — we have free paper bags at every bin, plus deposit-return mason jars for $1 each (refunded when you return them clean).

Are the bins really cheaper than the supermarket?

For most pantry staples, yes — 20–40% cheaper because you're not paying for packaging, marketing, or shipping a half-empty box. Specialty items (organic spices, single-origin) sit closer to supermarket price.

How fresh is the food in the bins?

Spices get refilled monthly, flours weekly, oats and grains in 5kg batches a few times a week. We keep the bin labels small on purpose so the food cycles fast.

Where does it all come from?

Most of the floor (grains, flours, lentils, oats, honey) is from prairie farms within 250km. Spices, nuts, dried fruit, and tea are single-origin from somewhere we trust — labelled bin-by-bin.
