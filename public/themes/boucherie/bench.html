The craft

Three generations behind the counter.

"Whole-animal butchery isn't a marketing line for us. It's how the shop has worked since 1962, because it's the only honest way to know what's in the case."

How we source.

We buy whole or half animals from a small handful of farms within 200 km of Your Store Name. We know each farm by name. We know what they feed, when they slaughter, and what the animal lived like.

That means our case looks different on a Tuesday than it does on a Friday — because we're cutting through an animal, not topping up trays from a wholesale list.

How we cure.

Our pâtés, terrines, saucissons and cured hams are made downstairs, not bought in. Recipes from the founder's grandmother, ingredient lists you could read in five seconds.

Salt, pepper, herbs, time. That's most of it.

How we sell.

Across a counter, by the gram, with eye contact. Tell us what you're cooking and we'll cut for it. We'd rather sell you the right cut at half the size than the wrong one in plastic wrap.

"You'll never leave with something we wouldn't cook ourselves."
